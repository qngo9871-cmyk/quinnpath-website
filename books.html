Books

Four works drawn from four decades of practice — each a different lens on the nature of mind, awareness, and the possibility of transformation.

I

Forty-Three Days of Fire:
A Journey Into The Hidden Universe

★★★★½ 30 reviews

An unflinching first-hand account of a forty-three day intensive retreat in the ancient monastery tradition of Myanmar. In this remarkable chronicle, Quinn Path documents what happens when a Western mind trained in Buddhist scholarship is pushed to the outermost edges of concentrated meditation practice.

The result is a book unlike any other in contemporary contemplative literature — neither romanticised nor sensationalised, but rigorously honest about the extraordinary capabilities of consciousness that emerge under conditions of sustained, disciplined attention.

II

Between Sets:
A Mindfulness Practice for Surfers

Mindfulness

Between the waves — in the pause, the stillness, the waiting — lies one of the most natural entry points into meditative awareness available to a modern person. Between Sets takes the ancient teachings of mindfulness and reframes them through the lived language of surf culture.

Written for surfers who have already tasted something wordless in the water, this book offers a structured path inward — using the ocean itself as both metaphor and teacher. No prior experience with meditation required.

III

Learning to Pay Attention:
A Short Introduction to Meditation

Introduction

Meditation is not complicated. It is, however, easy to approach in ways that make it feel that way. Learning to Pay Attention strips away the confusion and offers something rare: a genuinely simple, genuinely accurate introduction to what meditation actually is and how to do it.

Written by a practitioner with both scholarly grounding and decades of personal experience, this short book is the ideal starting point for anyone curious about the interior life.

IV

Morning Coffee: The Simple Daily Practice That Transforms Emotional Overwhelm into Spiritual Freedom

★★★★★ 1 review
Also in Chinese · 中文版

The contemplative life does not begin at a retreat centre. It begins in the kitchen, in the first quiet moment of the morning, before the day has demanded anything of you. Morning Coffee offers a single, simple practice — rooted in decades of retreat experience — that anyone can do, anywhere.

Translated into Chinese, this book has resonated across cultures as a practical and profound guide to meeting emotional difficulty with something larger than reactivity.
